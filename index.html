Emergancy Essentials

What to Pack in My Emergancy Go Bag


Food & Water

  1. Drinking water: minimum of 3L per person per day
  2. Non-perishable food: canned goods, trail mix, instant noodles, honey
  3. Water filters & Iodine: filter should handle at least 2,000 gallons

First Aid & Health

  1. First-aid kit: bandages/gauze, antiseptic wipes, adhesive tape, scissors
  2. Basic medicines: pain killers, antihistamines, prescribed medications
  3. Face masks: N95 or surgical
  4. Personal hygiene items: soap, toothbrush/paste, sanitary pads, alcohol

Safety & Tools

  1. Flashlight: solar or crank-powered
  2. Multi-tool or Swiss Army Card
  3. Survival whistle: 100 decibels or more
  4. Mathces/lighter: cover matches with paraffin wax or turpentine to waterproof
  5. Sturdy rope

Documents & Money

  1. Photocopies of IDs: passport, driver's license, birth certificate
  2. Emergancy contact list
  3. Cash: small bill or coins

Clothing & Shelter

  1. Clothes: clean and dry weather-appropriate, covers as much skin as possible
  2. Strudy Shoes
  3. Plastics: large garbage bags, waterproof mats
  4. Blanket or emergency thermal blanket

Comms and Extras

  1. Batteries and power bank: power bank should make use of solar energy
  2. Radio: solar, hand-crank, or battery-powered
  3. Local maps: GPS might fail
  4. Other items: small mirrors, sunglasses
